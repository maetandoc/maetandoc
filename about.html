About

I'm Mae Tandoc.
Pocket Partner.

Nice to meet you! My name is Mae. I am the owner and very first pocket partner. In the midst of all the data-driven algorithms and analytics involved in small businesses, I am an artist and storyteller at heart.

I specialize in working with entrepreneurs, and align myself with their visions to ensure that their clients and customers feel the same passion about their business as they do. 

So how did I get into this crazy world of helping small businesses? It all started with a little high school project called æ„›Ichigo Latte Made & Butler Cafe. I got the first taste of entrepreneurship when working with a team of ecletic, talented individuals who helped me create a brand from the ground up. It was my pride and joy.

Alas, we all went to college and collectively made the decision to disband. After initially going for a Pharmacy degree, I spent most of my days fuming over chemistry equations and drug interactions but my heart had always belonged to creating art, music, films, and telling stories. But life had always found a way to lead me to my purpose. So I decided to switch over to a Pharmaceutical and Healthcare Business degree (Yes, it's close enough to a normal business degree! My college only had science degrees!).

I worked as a freelance singer and model during that time so I had to learn everything from the ground up just to get a little bit of money here and there while I took on several internships related to my newly-minted degree choice. From social media to SEO to website building to sales and marketing, I learned a little bit of everything from hopping around all types of industries.

Heck, I even had a license to sell life and health insurance! (Sorry, insurance agencies - my license has expired.)

After graduating, I got "real" job! I worked as a project manager for a startup agency called NXTFactor that really honed my skills in digital marketing while I was blessed with the opportunity to work for huge restaurant clients like Chubby Cattle and The XPot. Guess those cafe skills really came to use now, huh?

Now, I am involved with freelancing as a virtual assistant after following my dream of starting an acting career.

I pride in the fact that gaining these seemingly unrelated experiences allow me to help others as an artist, a businesswoman, and just a plain, human being. Hard work, commitment, and creating genuine, human connections are values that I never lose sight of despite whatever life throws my way.
