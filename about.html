About

I'm Mae Tandoc.
Pocket Partner.

My name is Mae. I am the owner and very first pocket partner. In the midst of all the data-driven algorithms and analytics involved in small business and digital marketing, I am an artist and storyteller at heart.

I specialize in working with entrepreneurs, and align myself with their visions to ensure that their clients and customers feel the same passion about their business as they do. 

So how did I get into this crazy world of helping small businesses? Back in high school, my first t-shirt business venture, BABY♡DOLL. was a FLOP. I got my real first taste of entrepreneurship when I started 愛Ichigo Latte Made & Butler Cafe. Working with a team of ecletic, talented individuals who helped me create a brand from the ground up for almost 3 years, it became my pride and joy.

Alas, we all went to college and collectively made the decision to disband. After initially going for a 6 year Doctor of Pharmacy degree at the University of the Sciences, I spent most of my days fuming over chemistry equations and drug interactions although my heart had always belonged to creating art, music, films, and telling stories.

But life had always found a way to lead me to my purpose.

I decided to switch over to a Pharmaceutical and Healthcare Business degree (My college only had science degrees!) while attending an acting conservatory at Playhouse West to fulfill my creative needs.

I worked as a freelance singer and model during that time so I had to build my own brand from the ground up just to get a little bit of money here and there while taking on several internships related to my newly-minted degree choice.

I also helped out some of my friends in starting their own businesses and learning from their processes. From social media to SEO to website building to PR to sales to marketing, I learned a little bit of everything from hopping around all types of industries. Heck, I even had a license to sell life and health insurance at some point! (Sorry, insurance agencies - my license is expired.)

After graduation, I got a "real" job working as a digital marketing project manager for a company called NXTFactor. I was blessed with the opportunity to work for huge restaurant clients like Chubby Cattle and The XPot. Guess those cafe skills really came to use now, huh?

Now, I am involved with freelancing as a virtual assistant and social media manager for My Pocket Partner after pursuing my dream of working as a full-time actor. Interested in keeping up with my acting career? Check out maeclaire.com and you can see how I put all my digital marketing experiences to the test!

I pride in the fact that gaining these seemingly unrelated experiences allow me to help others as an artist, a businesswoman, and just a plain, human being. Hard work, commitment, and creating genuine, human connections are values that I never lose sight of despite whatever life throws my way.
